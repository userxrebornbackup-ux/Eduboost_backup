# Ethical experimentation guidelines for minors

Experiments must be educationally justified, low risk, privacy-preserving, and reversible.

## Rules

- No dark patterns, pressure mechanics, or manipulative scarcity.
- No experiment may reduce access to safety, privacy, support, or core learning value.
- Avoid experiments that shame, rank, or negatively label learners.
- Use pseudonymous assignment and minimal analytics.
- Guardian-facing copy changes must be reviewed for clarity and consent implications.
- Content/diagnostic experiments require educator review before broad rollout.

## Approved experiment classes

- Onboarding copy clarity.
- Diagnostic length within validated assessment constraints.
- Lesson explanation style where correctness and CAPS alignment are unchanged.
- Parent report format and readability.

## Prohibited experiment classes

- Hiding privacy controls.
- Nudging excessive use.
- Making support harder to access.
- Changing correctness, grading, or CAPS references without review.
